Theorem 8.4.2.

Let \(a, b, n\in \mathbb{Z}, n>1\text{.}\) The following statements are equivalent.

  1. \(\displaystyle n\mid a-b\)
  2. \(\displaystyle a\equiv b \mod n\)
  3. \(a=b+kn\) for some \(k\in \mathbb{Z}\)
  4. \(a\) and \(b\) have the same remainder when divided by \(n\)
  5. \(a \mod n = b \mod n\text{,}\) where here we refer to the remainder when dividing by \(n\text{.}\)
