Let \(a, b\) be even. Then by definition, \(a=2k\) for some \(k\in \mathbb{Z}\) and \(b=2j\) for some \(j\in \mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) (Note, we cannot use \(k\) for both \(a\) and \(b\) as they likely are two different numbers.) Then \(a+b=2k+2j=2(k+j)\) where \(k+y\in \mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) Which means \(a+b\) is even.
