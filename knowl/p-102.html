One of the thing we want to be able to do is to translate English (or mathematical) sentences into logical statements. We have to be careful about how common phrases are really hidden "and"s. For example, "\(p\) but \(q\)" is really \(p\wedge q\text{.}\) Similarly, "neither \(p\) nor \(q\)" is really \(\sim p \wedge \sim q\text{.}\)
