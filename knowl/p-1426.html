Since we are looking for sums of 11, our boxes can be pairs of numbers that sum to 11: \(\{1, 10\}, \{2, 9\}, \{3, 8\}, \{4, 7\}, \{5, 6\}\text{.}\) Then our objects are the numbers themselves. So we need the number of numbers to be greater than the number of boxes, which is 5. Thus, if we choose 6 numbers from \(A\text{,}\) we are guaranteed to have 2 from the same subset. This means with 6 numbers we must have two numbers that sum to 11.
