We can calculate combinations by first finding all the \(r\)-permuatiations of a set, then dividing by all the possible orderings of that subset: \(\frac{P(n, r)}{r!}\text{.}\) This can be rewritten to be the standard formula for calculating combinations:

\begin{equation*} \binom{n}{r}=\frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}\text{.} \end{equation*}
