Why can we change the assumption? Well, remenber how induction works, first we show \(P(1)\text{,}\) then the induction step gets us to \(P(2)\text{,}\) which then gets us to \(P(3)\text{,}\) etc. But once we get to, say, \(P(4)\text{,}\) we already know \(P(1), P(2), P(3)\text{.}\) So we may as well assume we have \(P(i)\) for everything up to \(P(k)\text{.}\)
