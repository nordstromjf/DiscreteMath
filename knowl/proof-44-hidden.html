By definition, \(\binom{n+1}{r}\) counts the subsets of \(r\) objects chosen from \(n+1\) objects.

Let \(S=\{x_1, x_2, \ldots x_{n+1}\}\text{,}\) a set of \(n+1\) objects. We can create all the subsets of \(r\) objects by choosing all \(r\) objects from \(\{x_1, \ldots, x_n\}\) or by choosing \(r-1\) out of \(\{x_1, \ldots, x_n\}\) and including \(x_{n+1}\text{.}\) Thus, we can count the subsets of \(r\) objects from \(n\) objects with \(\binom{n}{r}+\binom{n}{n-r}\text{.}\)
