Assume \(a=b+kn\) for some \(k\in \mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) By the Quotient-Remainder Theorem, \(a=qn+r\) with \(0\leq r< n\text{.}\) Thus, \(b+kn=qn+r\text{.}\) Solving for \(b\text{,}\) we get \(b=(q-k)n+r\text{.}\) But by uniquenes of the remainder, \(b\) has remainder \(r\text{.}\)
