There are other ways to prove existence. One common way is a proof by contradiction, which we will see later in this chapter. There are several examples of existence proofs in Calculus where it can be shown that something exists without constructing a specific value. For example, take a look at a proof of Rolle's Theorem, the Mean Value Theorem, the Extreme Value Theorem, or the Taylor Remainder Theorem (Calc II).
